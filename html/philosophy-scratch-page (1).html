My Favorite Philosophers

These thinkers have profoundly shaped my understanding of wisdom, virtue, and the examined life.

  1. Socrates (470-399 BCE)
    • Major Contribution: Developed the Socratic Method of questioning to stimulate critical thinking and illuminate ideas. Founded Western philosophy through dialectical inquiry.
    • Famous Quote: "The unexamined life is not worth living."
  2. Aristotle (384-322 BCE)
    • Major Contribution: Created formal logic and established numerous fields of study including ethics, politics, metaphysics, and biology. His concept of the "Golden Mean" shaped virtue ethics.
    • Famous Quote: "We are what we repeatedly do. Excellence, then, is not an act, but a habit."
  3. Marcus Aurelius (121-180 CE)
    • Major Contribution: Exemplified Stoic philosophy through his personal writings in "Meditations." Demonstrated how philosophy could be lived daily, even by a Roman Emperor.
    • Famous Quote: "You have power over your mind - not outside events. Realize this, and you will find strength."
  4. Friedrich Nietzsche (1844-1900)
    • Major Contribution: Challenged traditional morality and religion with concepts like the "Will to Power," the "Übermensch," and "Eternal Recurrence." Proclaimed "God is dead" and called for the revaluation of all values.
    • Famous Quote: "He who has a why to live can bear almost any how."
  5. Heraclitus (535-475 BCE)
    • Major Contribution: Proposed that change is the fundamental essence of the universe with his doctrine of flux: "Panta Rhei" (everything flows). Introduced the concept of the unity of opposites and the Logos.
    • Famous Quote: "No man ever steps in the same river twice, for it's not the same river and he's not the same man."
  6. Jesus of Nazareth (c. 4 BCE - c. 30 CE)
    • Major Contribution: Taught radical love, forgiveness, and compassion. Emphasized inner transformation and the Kingdom of God. His ethical teachings, including the Golden Rule and the Sermon on the Mount, profoundly influenced Western moral philosophy.
    • Famous Quote: "Love thy neighbor as thyself."
